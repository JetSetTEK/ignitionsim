Verdict first: **buy a [VKB Gladiator NXT EVO Space Combat Edition](/space/gear/vkb-gladiator-nxt-evo-space-combat-edition) pair and treat every step above it as optional.** Most boutique flight gear punishes you for starting cheap — your entry stick becomes e-waste the day you upgrade. VKB is the exception, because the value isn't a grip you carry forward so much as an *ecosystem* you grow inside: your VKBDevCfg profiles, your muscle memory, and a grip catalog that keeps expanding above you. That means the Gladiator you buy today is the bottom rung of a real ladder, not a dead end — and for most pilots, the bottom rung is the whole staircase.

This is that ladder, rung by rung, with the price of each step and — more importantly — the *trigger* that tells you it's time to take it. If you're not yet committed to VKB specifically, weigh it against the field in [the best beginner HOSAS setup under $300](/space/best-beginner-hosas-under-300) and the three-brand [VKB vs Virpil vs WinWing breakdown](/space/vkb-vs-virpil-vs-winwing-space-sticks-2026) first. This guide assumes you've decided on VKB and want to know exactly how far the road goes — and where to get off it.

## Step 1: The Gladiator pair — your foundation

Two [Gladiator NXT EVO "Space Combat Edition"](/space/gear/vkb-gladiator-nxt-evo-space-combat-edition) sticks (around $119 Standard or $139 Premium each — the Premium adds a rapid-fire trigger and an analog ministick) is the foundation everything else bolts onto. The Space Combat Edition's button layout is purpose-built for **Elite Dangerous** and **Star Citizen**, the gimbal runs contactless magnetic sensors that won't drift like cheap potentiometer sticks, and it's built as a *dedicated* left or right hand — so you buy one of each for a true HOSAS pair, not two of the same grip.

For most pilots, this rung is the whole staircase. A Gladiator pair will fly combat, exploration and trading for years without ever feeling like the limiting factor. Do not buy step 2 or 3 reflexively — buy them when a specific, nameable itch appears.

The one task you can't skip here is mounting. Two sticks with a light, compact base have enough leverage to walk across a desk under hard input, so plan for clamp mounts from day one rather than discovering the problem mid-dogfight.

## Step 2: The Omni-Throttle — analog thrust that stays put

The first itch most HOSAS pilots feel is cruise control. A self-centering left stick springs forward thrust back to zero, which is fine for a dogfight and maddening on a forty-minute supercruise.

The [Gladiator NXT EVO Omni-Throttle](/space/gear/vkb-gladiator-nxt-evo-omni-throttle) (around $149) fixes that. It's a Gladiator stick and grip mounted on an Omni-Throttle Adapter, so your off hand becomes a multi-axis thrust-and-strafe controller that can *hold* a position instead of springing back. It's VKB's answer to "I want a throttle, but I fly HOSAS" — you get set-and-forget forward thrust without giving up analog lateral strafe. Because it shares the Gladiator's magnetic sensors and self-centering twist, it slots into the same VKBDevCfg profiles you already built.

**Trigger to buy:** you've caught yourself wishing the left stick would just stay where you put it. If that's never bothered you, skip this rung entirely — it isn't a step you owe the ladder.

## Step 3: The Gunfighter Mk.IV base — the feel upgrade

This is the real upgrade, and it's a *feel* upgrade, not a capability one. Here's the honest engineering difference, straight from VKB's own comparison: the Gladiator uses a **fiberglass-infused pincer gimbal with fixed cams** and one spring per axis, while the [Gunfighter Mk.IV base](/space/gear/vkb-gunfighter-mk4-base) (around $289, base only) uses an **all-metal, cam-based gimbal** that takes two springs per cam from four included spring sets, across four cam profiles (Avia Hard/Soft and Space Hard/Soft). Translated: the Gladiator's centering and breakout feel is baked in, and the Gunfighter's is a dial you turn.

The Gunfighter also carries VKB's **Rev.C grip connector** — a round, gold-plated coupling that lets you rotate and swap between five interchangeable Gunfighter-family grips in minutes. That's the grip interchangeability that makes the top of the ladder modular. One caveat worth stating plainly, because it's easy to assume otherwise: your existing *Gladiator* grip does not drop onto a Gunfighter base. What moves between bases is the Gunfighter/Rev.C grip catalog and your software profiles — not the Gladiator's own grip. So the staged upgrade is "buy a Gunfighter base plus a Rev.C grip, run it on your aiming hand, keep the Gladiator on the off hand," rather than "cannibalize the Gladiator."

Done that way, the climb is genuinely incremental: one Gunfighter base under the aiming hand, a mixed rig for a while, and a second base only if and when you decide the translation hand needs it too.

**Trigger to buy:** you can articulate, specifically, what you dislike about the Gladiator's centering or breakout feel. If you can't name the complaint, you don't need the fix yet.

## Step 4: The MCG Pro grip — the flagship topping

The top rung is a combat grip for that Gunfighter base. The [MCG Pro (Modern Combat Grip)](/space/gear/vkb-mcg-pro-grip) (around $136 grip-only; the Ultimate roughly $234) packs six analog axes plus two ministicks, a dual-stage trigger, a metal brake lever on a contactless sensor, and a 25mm-adjustable palm rest for all-day comfort. It's designed for the bind-heavy combat **Star Citizen** throws at you, and on the Gunfighter base it's the best thing VKB makes for a right hand.

Two honest caveats. First, it's a grip *only* — useless without a Gunfighter base under it, so it's never a standalone purchase. Second, the MCG's combat-shaped ergonomics suit an aiming hand far better than a symmetric HOSAS off hand, so most pilots run one MCG on the aiming stick and something lighter on the translation hand. And you almost certainly want the Pro, not the pricier Ultimate: the Pro already covers six analog axes, and the Ultimate's extra modules are for the deepest bind hoarders.

This rung is unapologetically for enthusiasts. It's a want. The performance delta over a well-tuned Gladiator pair is real but small — measured in grams of grip feel and the satisfaction of flagship hardware, not in kills per hour.

## The honest stopping points

Here's where most pilots actually get off the ladder:

- **Most stop at Step 1.** A Gladiator pair is enough, full stop.
- **Long-haul and exploration pilots add Step 2.** The Omni-Throttle earns its keep on anyone who logs real cruise time.
- **Combat-focused enthusiasts reach Step 3.** Adjustable cams matter when fine aim is your whole game.
- **Step 4 is for people who want the best, knowing the gap is small.** No shame in it — just go in clear-eyed.

## The setup tax nobody prices in

The hardware ladder has a shadow: two things that aren't in the SKU list but are absolutely part of a working VKB rig.

**Mounting.** Every stick here has a light, compact baseplate that will slide under aggressive combat input, and two loose sticks walk in opposite directions. A [Monstertech table mount](/space/gear/monstertech-table-mount) per stick locks each one rigid at an ergonomic angle. A HOSAS needs two, and they're a buy-once item — budget the mounts when you budget the sticks, not after your first firefight sends a stick skating across the desk.

## The buy order

1. **Buy the Gladiator pair first** — one dedicated left, one dedicated right. This is the foundation and, for most, the destination.
2. **Buy two Monstertech mounts in the same cart** — light bases slide, and two loose sticks walk apart.
3. **Add a Stream Deck before any fancier base** — the 200-plus binds hurt more than a spring feel.
4. **Learn VKBDevCfg** — an evening now saves a season of "why does this feel off."
5. **Add the Omni-Throttle** only when cruise control genuinely nags at you.
6. **Step to a Gunfighter base** only when you can name what you dislike about the Gladiator's cam feel — then upgrade the aiming hand first.
7. **Top it with an MCG Pro** only when "the best VKB makes" is a thing you actively want, knowing the gap is small.

## Verdict

Buy the **Gladiator NXT EVO pair**, mount both sticks, add a Stream Deck for the binds, and treat everything above that as optional. The reason to choose VKB over a dead-end budget stick isn't that you'll definitely climb the ladder — it's that the *option* costs you nothing, because the ecosystem, your profiles and your muscle memory all come with you. Add the **Omni-Throttle** when cruise control nags, step to a **Gunfighter Mk.IV base** when you can name what you dislike about the Gladiator's cam feel, and reach for the **MCG Pro** only when "the best VKB makes" is a thing you actively want.
