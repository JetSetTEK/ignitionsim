Today, three sensors under a grand give you honest numbers and connect to a real simulator. But here is the part the buyer's guides bury: the units people *remember* being cheap — the FlightScope Mevo, the SkyTrak, the Bushnell — have all drifted above $1,000 in 2026. The genuinely-sub-$1K field today is three units, and the right one depends entirely on your room and your software. ## The Honest Sub-$1,000 Field in 2026

Three units genuinely own this tier today:

- **Rapsodo MLM2PRO** (~$699.99) — dual-camera fusion plus Doppler radar, measured spin with RPT balls, and *official* GSPro direct connect since the 2025 PGA Show. Phone-tethered, indoor and outdoor. The richest data set under a grand.
- **Square Golf** (~$699) — the only true photometric *camera* unit under $1,000. Sits beside the ball, so it thrives in shallow indoor rooms, and it's genuinely good at putting and short game. Indoor-only.
- **Garmin Approach R10** (~$500, often lower) — Doppler radar, ~16 data points, the only unit here with a true on-device simulator, and Garmin's 43,000-course library. The cheapest credible door and the outdoor value king.

What is *not* here anymore, and why it matters: FlightScope killed the sub-$1K Mevo+ and its current **Mevo Gen 2 starts at $1,299**. The **Bushnell Launch Pro** is ~$2,499. The **SkyTrak+** clears near $2,000 and the original SkyTrak now lives on the used market. Great gear — just not honest sub-$1,000 buys in 2026. I cover them below as step-ups so you know exactly where the line is.

## Rapsodo MLM2PRO: The Data-Honest Pick

The MLM2PRO is the one I'd buy if I cared about *measured* numbers. At roughly **$699.99** it adds dual-camera fusion to radar, which gets you genuinely measured spin rather than an estimate, and the camera component makes it more forgiving indoors than pure radar. The headline development is the **official GSPro direct connect** announced at the 2025 PGA Show — that collapsed the price of legitimately GSPro-ready hardware toward $700, which is a big deal. It also runs E6 Connect across a 30,000-plus course library and is genuinely portable, indoor and outdoor friendly.

The honest asterisks: measured spin rate and spin axis need **RPT-marked balls** (Callaway Chrome Soft X RPT or Titleist Pro V1 RPT), and the best software features live behind **Rapsodo Premium** — about **$199.99/yr**, or a one-time lifetime unlock around **$599.99**. If your room is tight and you want the best data per dollar, this is the pick.

## Square Golf: The Indoor Camera Sleeper

If your room is a shallow garage or basement corner, the **Square Golf** launch monitor is the one nobody talks about enough. At about **$699** it is the *only* true photometric camera unit under $1,000 in 2026, and because it sits *beside* the ball instead of behind it, it doesn't demand the rear depth that radar quietly needs. It tracks putting, chipping, and flop shots — short-game data that is genuinely rare at this price — and it avoids a Square annual fee, using a token model for its own course play instead. Third-party software (GSPro, E6, Awesome Golf) is separate and worth confirming before purchase.

The honest limits: it is **indoor-only**, so range and outdoor buyers should look at the R10 or MLM2PRO, and it is a newer ecosystem with less long-term owner history than Garmin. But for a dedicated indoor practice corner where short game matters, it is the first budget pick I check.

## Garmin R10: The Cheapest Credible Start

The R10 is still the unit I point most first-timers to when budget and portability come first. For around **$500** — often less on sale — you get a genuinely capable Doppler radar sensor, ~16 data points, and the only true *on-device* simulator in this class, meaning you can get rolling without immediately committing to a gaming PC. It connects to GSPro and E6 through middleware for the big-screen experience, it is dead-simple to carry to the range, and the Garmin Golf membership (about **$99.99/yr** or **$9.99/mo**) unlocks a 43,000-plus course library and Home Tee Hero rounds.

The honest catch is radar's nature: it wants **depth behind you** to read full ball flight, which is the [whole radar-vs-photometric story](/golf/radar-vs-photometric-launch-monitor). In a shallow indoor room it extrapolates from less data and gets less reliable. Spin is estimated, not measured. Five years on, the budget market has caught up to the R10 indoors — but for outdoor range work and the cheapest honest start, it is still the value king.

## The Used-Market Wildcard: Original SkyTrak

There is a fourth path if you are willing to shop carefully: the **original SkyTrak** on the used market. ## Warnings Before You Buy

- **Radar needs room depth.** The R10 (and any radar unit) wants several feet behind the hitter to read full flight. In a shallow room it guesses. Measure your depth before you buy radar — this is the single most common budget regret.
- **The subscription is part of the price.** GSPro is about **$250/yr** and needs a Windows gaming PC. Rapsodo Premium is ~$199.99/yr (or ~$599.99 lifetime). Garmin Golf is ~$99.99/yr. The sticker is the down payment, not the bill.
- **"Under $1,000" is a moving target.** The SkyTrak, Bushnell, and FlightScope units people assume are cheap have all crossed $1,000 in 2026. Don't anchor on old reviews.
- **Indoor camera units want consistent lighting.** Square Golf and SkyTrak are photometric — inconsistent basement lighting degrades reads.
- **Measured spin has a ball tax.** The MLM2PRO's measured spin needs RPT-marked balls, or your spin numbers are estimates dressed up as data.

## The Buy Order

1. Measure your room depth *before* you fall in love with a sensor — depth decides radar vs. camera.
2. Shallow indoor-only room → Square Golf (camera, beside the ball) or a clean used SkyTrak.
3. Some depth or you play outdoors too → Rapsodo MLM2PRO for the richest data, Garmin R10 for the cheapest start.
4. Budget the software layer: GSPro (~$250/yr) plus a Windows PC, or lean on the free on-device paths first.
5. Confirm your target simulator software supports the sensor *before* you check out — compatibility quietly rules units in or out.
6. Buy RPT balls if you chose the MLM2PRO and actually want measured spin.

## So Which One?

- **Best measured data and GSPro-ready out of the box:** Rapsodo MLM2PRO (~$700). Fusion plus direct connect makes it the data-honest choice, and it's forgiving in tighter rooms.
- **Best for a shallow indoor bay and short game:** Square Golf (~$699). The only true camera unit under a grand, sitting beside the ball where radar can't fit.
- **Cheapest credible start and best outdoors:** Garmin R10 (~$500). The on-device sim is genuinely unique at this price, and radar loves the range.
- **Willing to shop used carefully:** original SkyTrak — camera data on the cheap, if the account transfers cleanly.
- **Can stretch past $1,000:** the FlightScope Mevo Gen 2 ($1,299) is the no-fee step-up worth waiting a paycheck for.
