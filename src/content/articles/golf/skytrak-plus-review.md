## What you are actually buying in 2026

The SkyTrak+ is a **photometric-class launch monitor with a combined camera and dual Doppler radar**. It sits beside the ball — not behind you, not overhead — which makes it friendly to tighter indoor rooms where a radar unit would want more depth. If you are unsure why placement matters this much, our [radar vs photometric explainer](/golf/radar-vs-photometric-launch-monitor) breaks down how each technology reads a shot and what room geometry it needs.

The hardware upgraded the original SkyTrak in two ways that still matter: it added radar data alongside the camera for richer physics, and it delivers **club data natively** without face stickers. That last point removes a recurring annoyance that still dogs cheaper units.

The catch you cannot ignore: **SkyTrak's own store lists the SkyTrak+ as no longer available**, with remaining inventory selling around **$1,795–$1,995** against the old **$2,995** list. So the value proposition has inverted. You are not paying a premium for the current unit; you are getting last-generation hardware at a clearance price, with the trade-offs that end-of-life status brings.

## The subscription truth, updated for the new tiers

The good news survives: out of the box you get a **free Basic tier** — the SkyTrak driving range, PinSeeker tournaments, and shot-data recording — with no annual fee. Your practice numbers are yours. That is still rare at this price.

The asterisk got bigger. SkyTrak renamed and restructured its plans, and in 2026 the ladder looks like this:

| Membership | Approx. price/year | What it unlocks |
|---|---|---|
| **Basic** | Free | Driving range, PinSeeker tournaments, shot recording |
| **Essential** | ~$129.99 | Bag mapping, skills assessments, structured/randomized practice |
| **Core (Foresight)** | ~$299.99 | Course Play with the Foresight library (30+ courses) |
| **Core (Trackman)** | ~$349.99 | Course Play with the Trackman library (30+ courses) |
| **Elite** | ~$599.99 | Both libraries (69+ courses), Pebble Beach, Royal Portrush, member discounts |

So "no subscription" is honest for a practice unit, and "most home users budget Core at ~$300/year" is the honest follow-up if you want to play actual courses inside SkyTrak's software. That is a real recurring line item — treat it like one.

## The software change you must verify before buying

This is the single most important 2026 update, and it is easy to miss because older reviews still list it as a strength.

The SkyTrak+ built part of its reputation on **software freedom** — the rare TGC 2019 support alongside GSPro, E6 Connect, and Awesome Golf. That framing is now stale for new buyers. Across retailer write-ups and owner forums, the consistent picture is that **SkyTrak has stepped back from officially supporting third-party simulators for new customers**, steering them into its native Course Play platform (built on Foresight and Trackman course libraries) instead.

What that means in practice:

- **Existing owners** appear to be grandfathered into their established GSPro/E6 connections.
- **New buyers** are pointed at Course Play, not an official GSPro/E6/TGC hookup.
- **The community runs unofficial bridges** (an OpenSkyPlus-style connector using GSPro's open API, shared through Discord) that owners report working well — but they are unsupported and can break with a firmware or app change.

I am flagging this rather than asserting a clean yes/no, because it is exactly the kind of policy that shifts and varies by unit and retailer. **If a native GSPro connection is a hard requirement for you, confirm it in writing with the seller before you buy — check current.** Do not assume the old "software freedom" pitch still applies to a unit you buy today.

## Accuracy and the competitive picture

Reviewers consistently put the SkyTrak+ a notch below true fitting-grade units like the **Foresight GC3** (~$6,999) on club metrics, while matching much of their ball-data quality for a fraction of the cost. In head-to-head testing against a Trackman, owners generally report ball speed, carry, and spin landing in line, with the honest caveats being **no angle-of-attack reading**, barebones putting data, and outdoor use that wants a mat rather than natural grass. For a home bay where you want honest numbers and realistic ball flight — not a launch-monitor-for-a-fitting-studio — that trade is exactly right.

## SkyTrak+ vs ST MAX: the real decision

Since the SkyTrak+ is discontinued, the honest live comparison is no longer "SkyTrak+ or a rival" — it is "closeout SkyTrak+ or the in-production ST MAX."

SkyTrak shipped the **ST MAX** at **$2,995** as a refinement of the SkyTrak+, not a reinvention. Multiple hands-on reviews are blunt about this: the **core ball-and-club accuracy is identical**, and the **shot delay of about 1–2 seconds carries over from the SkyTrak+ generation** — it is not dramatically faster. What the MAX actually adds is a **faster processor, dual USB-C ports** (simultaneous charge and data), and a **GOLFTEC-style speed-training system**, plus a fresh charcoal design and the fact that it is the current, fully warrantied unit.

Note one correction to a common myth: the roughly **40% larger hitting zone arrived with the SkyTrak+** (over the original SkyTrak) and simply carries into the ST MAX. It is not a MAX-exclusive upgrade.

So the practical guidance:

- Buy the **ST MAX** if you want the in-production unit, full warranty coverage, dual USB-C, and the speed-training extras — and you would rather not own hardware that is already end-of-life.
- Buy the **closeout SkyTrak+** if you want the exact same accuracy for up to ~$1,000 less, can live with single-USB and no speed training, and accept that it is discontinued.

Neither is a wrong answer. The wrong answer is paying full ST MAX money expecting a big accuracy jump, or buying the SkyTrak+ at closeout without checking the software situation first.

1. Settle your **software plan first** — Course Play (and which membership tier), or a rival with a conventional GSPro/E6 path. This is now the decision that should drive the whole purchase.
2. Confirm, in writing with the retailer, whether a **new** SkyTrak unit will connect to any third-party software you require.

4. Budget the **SkyTrak membership** you'll actually use (most home players: Core, ~$300/year).

## The verdict

The SkyTrak+ still reads the honest ball flight that earned its following, and at a closeout price near $1,795–$1,995 the *hardware* is a genuine deal. But 2026 changed the story in two ways older reviews miss: the unit is **discontinued**, and SkyTrak has **pulled official third-party software support for new buyers** in favor of a paid Course Play platform. So the recommendation splits. If you want the current, supported, warrantied SkyTrak, buy the **ST MAX** and know you're paying for support and extras, not an accuracy leap. If you want the same numbers for less and you've settled your software plan, the **closeout SkyTrak+** is a smart buy — with your eyes open.
