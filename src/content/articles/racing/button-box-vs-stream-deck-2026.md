There's a point in every sim racer's life where the wheel runs out of buttons. You're in an endurance race juggling brake bias, traction control, ABS, fuel mix, lights, wipers, the pit limiter and three MFD pages, and you've got eleven inputs to map fifteen functions onto. That's when people start shopping for a **button box** or a **Stream Deck** — and the internet immediately tells them two opposite things.

The good news: the decision is mostly settled by one variable. The rest is nuance. Let me give you the rule first, then the detail, because if you race in VR the rest of this article barely matters.

## The rule: it's about whether you can see the screen

A **Stream Deck** is a grid of small LCD keys whose labels change with context. Its entire superpower is **visual** — the keys show you what they do, and they re-label themselves per car and per game. Take away your ability to look at it and you've thrown away the whole point.

So:

- **If you race in VR**, you **cannot see a Stream Deck**, full stop. A headset blanks out anything in the real room. Muscle memory on **physical switches** — a button box you find by feel — is the only thing that works. **Buy a button box.**
- **If you race on triple monitors or a single screen**, you can glance at a Stream Deck, and its context labels become a real advantage over memorising what an unlabeled toggle does. **A Stream Deck is genuinely compelling.**

That one fork decides it for most people. Everything below is for the buyers on the fence or the ones who want both.

## What each one is actually good at

| | Button box | Stream Deck |
|---|---|---|
| **Find blind (VR)** | Yes — physical switches | No — screen invisible in headset |
| **Rotary encoders** | Yes (brake bias, TC, ABS dials) | No |
| **Toggle / latching switches** | Yes (ignition, pumps, lights) | No (momentary press only) |
| **Number of functions** | Fixed by physical count | Near-unlimited via pages/folders |
| **Per-car / per-game labels** | No (static engraving or memory) | Yes — changes contextually |
| **Tactile confirmation** | Strong — you feel the click | Weak — flat key press |
| **SimHub / macros** | Via mapped inputs | Deep, with live telemetry on keys |
| **Best for** | VR, dials, switch feel | Visible screens, macros, info display |

The **button box** wins on the things that benefit from being a real, physical control: **rotary encoders** for stepped functions (brake bias up/down, TC level, ABS, fuel mix) and **toggle switches** for on/off states (ignition, fuel pump, lights). A real detented dial you can spin without looking is simply better for adjusting brake bias mid-stint than tapping a flat key.

The **Stream Deck** wins on **flexibility and information**: more functions than any box via pages and folders, labels that rewrite themselves so you're never guessing, and deep **SimHub** integration that can even display live telemetry — lap delta, fuel, tyre temps — right on the keys. For a monitor racer who likes macros and dashboards, that's hard to beat.

## The hybrid: Race Deck and running both

You don't actually have to choose. A **Race Deck** is just a Stream Deck mounted in a sim-racing case so it bolts neatly to your rig instead of sliding around the desk — same hardware, same software, cleaner integration. It's compatible across Stream Deck revisions.

And plenty of serious rigs run **both**: a button box for the rotaries and toggles your hands need to find blind, and a Stream Deck for the long tail of macros, telemetry pages and rarely-used functions. If your wheel already carries the core racing inputs and you're only adding panels for the overflow, a small box plus a deck covers nearly everything — though at that point you're well past the "out of buttons" threshold and into enthusiast territory.

## When to buy either one (and when not to)

Here's the honest part: **most people don't need either yet.** A modern GT wheel like the [MOZA RS V2](/racing/gear/moza-rs-v2) or [Fanatec McLaren GT3](/racing/gear/fanatec-mclaren) already carries the buttons, paddles and a rotary or two that cover sprint racing. You add a control panel when you genuinely **run out of inputs** — which in practice means:

- **Endurance racing** with full pit functions, fuel mix and stint management
- **Sims with deep MFDs** (iRacing's black boxes, ACC's pit pages) where you're constantly paging
- **Multi-class or rally** where lights, wipers and stage functions pile on top of the racing basics

If you're a sprint-race-only GT driver, your wheel is enough and the money belongs [further up the upgrade path](/racing/smart-upgrade-path) — on pedals or your base. Buying a Stream Deck "to look pro" while your wheel still has three unmapped buttons is the classic accessory-drawer purchase.

## What the community says

The forums have converged on exactly the VR fork above: VR racers are near-unanimous that a Stream Deck is useless in a headset and a physical box is the answer, while monitor racers rave about Stream Deck labels and SimHub telemetry keys. The other consistent note is from endurance and iRacing crowds — once you're managing pit functions and black boxes, *some* extra control surface stops being optional. The bikeshed argument is always button box versus Stream Deck; the real answer is usually "what do you actually run out of, and can you see the screen."

## Who should skip both

Sprint-only GT and formula drivers whose wheel still has spare inputs. VR drivers who haven't yet wanted more than their wheel offers (and if you do want more, you already know it's a box, not a deck). Anyone whose budget hasn't yet reached a solid base, [pedals](/racing/pedals-over-power) and a rigid rig — control panels are a refinement, not a foundation.

## The verdict

**Race in VR → buy a button box.** Physical switches you find by feel are the only thing that works in a headset, and rotaries for brake bias and TC are worth it on their own. **Race on a visible screen and love macros or telemetry → a Stream Deck (or rig-mounted Race Deck)** gives you near-unlimited, self-labelling functions a box can't match. **Want the best of both → run a small button box for the blind-find controls and a Stream Deck for everything else.** And if your wheel still has empty buttons, the right answer this month is **neither** — finish [the rest of your rig](/racing/configurator) first and come back when you've genuinely run out of inputs.
