# IgnitionSim Product Representation Audit

Audit date: July 24, 2026
Scope: public product catalog, certified articles, game hubs, category bays, Amazon coverage, and the adjoining products required to build a working simulator.

## Executive Finding

IgnitionSim has enough individual products to look broad, but several categories still do not represent the complete ownership system. Racing is the clearest example: it has deep wheelbase and pedal coverage, yet comparatively thin coverage of the software, PC, telemetry, mounting adapters, USB/power, audio/comms, cooling, and replacement parts that make those controls usable.

The corrective rule is now part of the Gold Standard:

> A category is complete only when a reader can choose, install, power, connect, calibrate, use, recover, and maintain a full simulator from our coverage.

## Racing

### What is strong

- Direct-drive wheelbases across entry, middle, and flagship torque.
- Load-cell, hydraulic, and active-pedal comparisons.
- Cockpit rigidity, wheel stands, fixed steel frames, and aluminum profile.
- Tactile feedback, haptics, motion, ultrawide, triples, and VR.
- Console compatibility warnings and several exact Amazon bundles.

### What is overrepresented

- Twenty-three wheelbases versus only a small number of fully specified complete stations.
- Multiple articles repeat the 9-12 Nm sweet-spot verdict without always carrying the reader through brake, frame, display, power, and software.
- Similar product-first cards can make the category feel like a catalog even when the advice is sound.

### What is missing or thin

1. **Software layer:** iRacing, LMU, ACC, AC EVO, rFactor 2, Automobilista 2, BeamNG, Dirt Rally, and console titles need a coherent build-around map.
2. **PC and platform:** CPU/GPU targets by triples, 49-inch ultrawide, 57-inch ultrawide, and VR; console licensing; storage and network.
3. **Display installation:** monitor stands, VESA capacity, eye distance, bezel handling, DisplayPort/HDMI limits, and GPU output planning.
4. **Telemetry and race controls:** DDUs, flag displays, button boxes, Stream Decks, radios, headsets, and overlays as one system.
5. **Discipline-specific touchpoints:** round, formula, GT, rally, NASCAR, and truck rims; shifter and handbrake paths; sequential versus H-pattern.
6. **Mounting and adapters:** quick releases, front mounts, side mounts, wheel decks, seat brackets, pedal plates, slider kits, shifter arms, and keyboard trays.
7. **USB and power:** powered hubs, direct-port rules, extension limits, grounding, selective suspend, cable labels, surge protection, and profile recovery.
8. **Comfort and endurance:** airflow, wind simulation, seat pressure, hydration, driver swaps, audio/comms, and easy egress.
9. **Maintenance and service:** replacement elastomers, springs, cables, bearings, power supplies, quick releases, cleaning, firmware rollback, and warranty path.
10. **Complete Amazon carts:** validated starter, committed PC, console, endurance, and VR stacks with every required companion part named.

### Immediate corrective work

- Published: **Best Sim Racing Software and Community Rigs 2026**, which joins software, controls, cockpit, display, USB/power, installation, calibration, and race rehearsal.
- Next Tier A: **Complete Sim Racing PC and Display Map 2026**.
- Next Tier A: **GT vs Formula vs Rally Controls: Rim, Shifter, Handbrake, and Button Layouts**.
- Next Tier A: **Sim Rig USB, Power, and Cable Reliability Bible**.
- Next Tier B: **Endurance Rig Comfort, Cooling, Comms, and Driver-Swap Guide**.
- Next Tier B: **Quick Releases, Mounts, Sliders, and the Adapters Your Rig Still Needs**.

## Golf

### What is strong

- Launch monitors from budget radar through premium camera systems.
- Room geometry, projectors, mats, screens, enclosures, and software.
- Several complete room-budget ladders.

### What is missing or thin

- PC, network, connector, and software-subscription topology by monitor.
- Putting surfaces, cups, cameras, alignment tools, and short-game practice.
- Lighting, glare, ceiling and wall protection, acoustic treatment, and HVAC.
- Consumables: tees, radar balls, metallic dots, impact-screen care, turf inserts, and projector filters.
- Installation labor, enclosure assembly, cable routes, electrical load, and recovery procedures.
- Exact room stacks that distinguish “included” from “still required.”

## Flight

### What is strong

- Beginner controls, yokes, HOTAS, throttles, rudders, and several premium comparisons.
- MSFS and DCS build-around editorial coverage.

### What is missing or thin

- GA, airliner, helicopter, bush, and military station maps as separate systems.
- Avionics panels, radio stacks, autopilot panels, switch panels, and instrument displays.
- Desk and cockpit mounts, chair control, keyboard/mouse access, and view management.
- Powered USB, networked panels, firmware order, device identifiers, and profile backup.
- Head tracking versus VR, audio/comms, cooling, and long-flight ergonomics.
- Spares and service plans for throttles, potentiometers, springs, clamps, and power supplies.

## Space

### What is strong

- HOTAS, HOSAS, beginner controls, and premium stick/throttle comparisons.
- Star Citizen and Elite Dangerous control-language coverage.

### What is missing or thin

- Complete game-specific stations for Star Citizen, Elite, X4, and space-combat titles.
- Desk mounts, center versus side stick, pedals, omnithrottle adapters, button decks, and keyboard access.
- Binding maps, device-order recovery, USB topology, and post-patch restoration.
- PC/display/VR requirements, head tracking, audio/comms, chairs, and long-session ergonomics.
- Replacement springs, cams, dampers, grips, cables, and service accessibility.

## Marine

### What is strong

- A distinctive editorial lane, marine software, VR sailing, and DIY throttle/helm thinking.

### What is missing or thin

- An honest complete off-the-shelf starter station.
- Helm/wheel, twin throttles, rudder, button panels, displays, seating, audio, and comms as one bridge.
- Mounting plates, adapters, microcontrollers, axis mapping, and labeled wiring.
- Large-screen and multi-display planning.
- Clear boundaries between entertainment simulation, training software, DIY building, and commercial bridge systems.
- Enough exact Amazon-available components to support a dependable revenue path.

## Cross-Sim Gaps

These are the highest-leverage missing systems because they serve multiple bays:

1. PC and display selector by workload.
2. USB, power, grounding, extension, and cable-recovery guide.
3. Mounting, seating, reach, and service-access guide.
4. Audio, communications, cooling, and comfort.
5. Haptics and motion by useful signal rather than spectacle.
6. Network and software-license recovery.
7. Maintenance, consumables, replacement parts, and warranty strategy.
8. Complete build sheets with “included / required / optional / avoid” status.

## Publishing Gate Going Forward

Before a new product guide is certified:

- State which part of the complete system it represents.
- Name every required adjoining part that is not in the box.
- Show product, installed-use, box/manual, and curator-action evidence when available.
- Include installation order, calibration, common failure, and recovery.
- Use exact Amazon product links for every retail-style product card.
- Update the relevant bay, game hub, stream, curator surface, related guides, sitemap, RSS, and homepage when the article is strategically featured.
- Re-run this representation audit quarterly and after any cluster grows by ten products.
