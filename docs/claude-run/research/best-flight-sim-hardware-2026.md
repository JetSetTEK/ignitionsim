## Community pattern notes (paraphrased in our voice)
- "Right brand, wrong airplane" is the most-repeated r/flightsim regret — a yoke bought for someone who then falls for the A320, or a Warthog bought for civil GA. Name the airplane before the budget.
- New simmers over-buy panels and head tracking before owning decent primary controls, then wonder why the rig feels off. Climb the ladder in order (rung 1 before rung 5).
- Multi-panel builders learn the powered-USB-hub lesson the hard way: a device silently drops out mid-approach. Both Logitech Saitek and WinWing tell you to run stacks off a powered hub.

## Credible warnings / caveats
- USB power: a full yoke/throttle/pedals + panels stack out-draws PC ports → powered hub (~$60) once past two panels.
- Desk mounting: Honeycomb yokes and TCA Boeing yoke need a firm clamp; MOZA AY210 servos need a rigid 4-bolt mount/frame.
- Airbus vs Boeing ergonomics don't cross over: side-stick wrong for Boeing, yoke wrong for Airbus.
- Calibration: head-tracking curves, throttle detent tension, airliner autopilot-panel bindings (Fenix A320, PMDG 737) all take a session to dial in.
- Warthog price crept up from long-standing ~$500 to ~$580-600 — flagged "check current".
