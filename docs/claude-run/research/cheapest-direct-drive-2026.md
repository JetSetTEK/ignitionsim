## Community pattern notes (paraphrased in our voice)
- Recurring budget confession: people who bought the absolute floor and then got serious within a year wish they'd stretched to a 12Nm sweet-spot base. Nobody who bought the R12 first regrets not going cheaper.
- "Buy the load-cell brake before more torque" is the most repeated budget-tier regret. A 5Nm base + good brake out-laps a bigger base + potentiometer pedals.
- MOZA R5 is the consensus "best value bundle" among 2026 reviews; Cammus C5 is the consensus "cheapest real DD"; R3 is the cheapest Xbox path.

## Credible warnings / caveats
- Entry Fanatec bases (CSL DD, GT DD Pro) only reach 8Nm with the Boost Kit 180 — confirm the power supply in the box.
- 12Nm (R12) overpowers a desk clamp; needs a 4-bolt mount + rigid stand. The 3.9-5Nm bundles are fine on a sturdy desk clamp.
- Cammus / Thrustmaster entry paths are shallower than MOZA/Fanatec ladders — buying the floor then getting serious can mean replacing the whole kit (resale cycle).
- Console lock is a hard spec: Cammus C5, MOZA R5/R9/R12 are PC-only.
- Cammus ownership polish has historically trailed the spec sheet (minor sample defects).
