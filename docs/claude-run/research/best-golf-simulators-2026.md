## Community pattern notes (paraphrased in our voice)
- Most repeated r/golfsimulator regret is the projector shadow: a longer-throw home-theater projector mounted behind the hitter casts a silhouette on ball flight. Short-throw exists for exactly this; measure throw and swing arc together and dry-swing under the mount before drilling.
- Budget the mat like safety gear — a cheap mat transmits clubhead shock to joints and quietly ends the habit.
- Buyers under-budget recurring costs (GSPro annual, Bushnell subscription tiers); run the three-year total-cost-of-ownership number, not the sticker.

## Credible warnings / caveats
- Room fit dictates sensor tech before brand: radar wants depth, photometric suits shallow rooms, overhead needs 9-10 ft ceilings — an overhead unit is wrong in an 8.5 ft basement.
- Projector is a geometry purchase: throw ratio, offset, mount point, and enclosure depth must fit the room; use the manufacturer throw calculator and tape the mount point before drilling.
- Price the launch-monitor AND GSPro subscriptions into the build; a "cheaper" monitor with mandatory subs can cost more over three years.
- Hold back $500-$1,000 for the "we forgot this" line.
