## Community pattern notes (paraphrased in our voice)
- Garage-build regret order: nobody wishes they'd bought a fancier launch monitor first; they wish they'd fixed the screen bounceback, the sloped floor, and the winter cold before committing.
- Measure to the lowest obstruction, not the drywall — the opener rail is usually lowest right over the tee.
- Radar wants depth (deep bay); photometric fits shallow bays but wants consistent lighting.

## Credible warnings / caveats
- Radar (R10) needs 6–8 ft behind the hitter + ~15 ft room length; a shallow one-car garage degrades its read → push to photometric.
- Cold: R10 operates to 14°F but batteries/screens/condensation make climate control near-mandatory in real winters.
- Every garage slab slopes for drainage (~1"/8 ft); an unlevel stance skews stance + putts + launch data.
- Photometric cameras hate inconsistent/swinging light and bright side windows.
