## Community pattern notes (paraphrased in our voice)
- The two recurring gripes: TrackIR people grumble about the clip hassle (one more thing on your head, reflector snapping off); Tobii people grumble about eye-tracking drift (jittery when holding still, wandering under changing light).
- The most-repeated Tobii fix: crank eye-tracking sensitivity way down and lean on the head tracking — settles the "noisier than TrackIR" feel for GA flying.
- Clean tiebreaker in the debate: if you already wear a headset every flight, the TrackClip PRO clips right on and the clip "hassle" nearly vanishes.

## Credible warnings / caveats
- Lighting matters more for the Tobii (camera watches your face) — a bright window behind you is its worst enemy; TrackIR (IR reflector) shrugs off room light.
- Glasses/beards favor TrackIR (tracks the clip, not your face); Tobii works with most glasses but heavy reflections/dark lenses nag its eye tracking.
- Tobii eye tracking is single-screen ≤ ~30 in — not the pick for triple-screen or ultrawide.
- Software step per platform: on MSFS 2024 start TrackIR software before the sim; for Tobii, re-check the help article after big sim updates (default sensitivities have shipped wrong).
- Set a good neutral seat/view first and bind recenter to a reachable button — used every flight.
- If you fly in VR, skip both — the headset already tracks your head.
