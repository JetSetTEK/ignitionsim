## Community pattern notes (paraphrased in our voice)
- The recurring 2026 "direct drive feels notchy/vague" complaint is almost always a flexing desk lifting on transients, not the base — the fix is a rigid mount, not a firmware tweak.
- "You must have 8020 or you're not serious" snobbery overshoots; steel cockpits and folding stands serve many racers well within their torque band.
- "Any desk is fine if you don't crank the FFB" is a myth — detuning a strong base to protect furniture wastes the torque you bought.
- Folding-stand "supports up to XNm" numbers are survival ratings, not zero-flex ratings; the comfortable feel is typically a tier lower.

## Credible warnings / caveats
- Above ~8Nm a desk edge lifts and distorts FFB; at 10Nm+ a dedicated stand/cockpit is required; at 15Nm+ aluminum profile beats steel tube.
- Bolt-pattern mismatches between base and rig/wheel-deck are common — confirm before buying or plan an adapter plate.
- Mount pedals to the same rigid structure as the wheel; a load cell on a sliding plate throws away repeatability.
- Monitor arrays add leverage — cantilevering triples off the wheel deck can rock the rig; use a braced cross-member or separate stand.
- 8020 rigs are heavy (40–50kg with seat) and permanent; folding stands trade rigidity for storability.
