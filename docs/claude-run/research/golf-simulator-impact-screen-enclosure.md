## Community pattern notes (paraphrased in our voice)
- The recurring confession: builders pull the screen drum-tight for a crisp image and take a driver off the shin on day one. A screen needs give.
- The second half of that lesson: leave an air gap behind the screen; a screen flat against block/drywall has nowhere to send the ball but back.
- Side barrier netting is repeatedly cited as the cheapest insurance that prevents a four-figure repair.

## Credible warnings / caveats
- Bounceback is mostly an install problem: drum-tight tension, no air gap, and skipping the weighted bottom cable / releasable ties cause the real rebound.
- Aspect ratio + projector throw + room depth are one decision — mismatches cause black bars, gaps, or spillover; forcing a 16:9 projector into 4:3/1:1 can dramatically increase required throw.
- Side/ceiling baffles are mandatory, not optional — the shank finds the projector or the wall without them.
- Prices are promo- and screen-tier-dependent; quoted as ranges + check-current throughout.
