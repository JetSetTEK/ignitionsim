## Community pattern notes (paraphrased in our voice)
- The most repeated "load cell feels worse than my old pedals" thread ends the same way: they calibrated 100% brake to the absolute hardest stomp. Fix is to set max to a force you press incredibly hard to reach (~80% of a genuine hard press) so you rarely bottom out — critical in non-ABS cars.
- "Buy pedals before more torque" is the single most repeated upgrade regret across r/simracing and review sites — brake feel beats base grip for lap time for most drivers.
- A resting-foot brake drag of even 1% costs tenths per lap and overheats virtual rotors; a small deadzone fixes it.

## Credible warnings / caveats
- A load cell only pays off on a rigid mount with the heel plate used; a flexing pedal deck throws away the repeatability.
- Load-cell calibration is a real setup step, not a formality — out-of-box max is often set unusably hard.
- Hydraulic brakes (Simagic P2000, Asetek Invicta) are fluid systems requiring occasional bleeding/seal care; load cells have none.
- Active pedals are priced per unit and deliver marginal lap-time gain for the money — luxury/novelty, not need.
- USB brand-agnostic connection (MOZA/Simagic/Heusinkveld/Asetek) means the brake survives every future wheelbase — don't buy base-locked.
