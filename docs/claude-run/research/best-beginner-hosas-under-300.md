## Community pattern notes (paraphrased in our voice)
- "Buy both sticks in the same cart" is the most-repeated beginner regret: HOSAS sticks ship as dedicated left/right units and the off-hand variant is perpetually out of stock.
- Recurring r/hotas confession: nobody regrets the VKB Gladiator; jumping straight to a Gunfighter pair without flying a Gladiator first earns the "Gladiator was good enough" admission.
- WinWing Ursa Minor repeatedly framed as the 2024-2025 "budget champion" — magnetic sensors plus in-stick haptics VKB skips at the price; SimAppPro is the consistent weak point.
- Newcomers keep landing on the Logitech X52/X56 while searching for a cheap space rig, not realizing they're one-box HOTAS units that can't become a HOSAS.

## Credible warnings / caveats (in-article as warning callouts)
- **Binding six axes is the real setup cost** — hardware is plug-and-play; mapping two sticks by hand is an evening's work (200+ binds in Star Citizen).
- **Desk space is not optional** — two bases + two mounts + keyboard/mouse; measure the desk first.
- **Ambidextrous / left-mappable is make-or-break** — a right-hand-only stick or a single-box HOTAS cannot serve as the off hand (why Logitech X52/X56 are excluded).
- **Plan the upgrade path** — T.16000M is a dead end by design; Ursa Minor and Gladiator build into ecosystems. Buying cheap then boutique costs more than buying boutique once.
