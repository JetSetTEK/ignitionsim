# IgnitionSim Reference Article Gold Standard v2

**Locked 2026-07-15.** This is the operational standard for every new
IgnitionSim cornerstone, buyer map, comparison, game review, installation
guide, and major refresh. It translates Puzzlewick's reference-article system
for an older, engineering-minded simulator audience.

Reference implementations:

- `simucube-activepedal-pro-vs-moza-mbooster-2026` for product comparison,
  installed proof, setup tax, and a blunt buying verdict;
- `foresight-gcquad-vs-gc3-vs-falcon-2026` for room fit and system thinking;
- `thrustmaster-sol-r2-vs-sol-r4-hosas-hotas-2026` for a current-product
  launch, real hardware proof, and space-sim control mapping.

The standard is a process, not a reusable page template. Repeating the same
layout, curator pose, dark workshop, or product cutout is a failure even when
all required fields are present.

## 1. The target reader

The primary reader is a Gen X or older-millennial builder who has money for a
good simulator and limited patience for hype, incompatible parts, weak mounts,
subscription surprises, and a lost weekend.

They normally arrive with one of five jobs:

1. Decide whether an expensive component is worth buying now.
2. Understand what else the component forces them to buy or rebuild.
3. Confirm that the room, rig, PC, console, software, power, and mounts agree.
4. See how the product looks installed and what ownership actually feels like.
5. Get an honest path from starter build to serious build without buying twice.

The target feeling is:

> I can picture the finished rig, I understand the system around the part, I
> know what will annoy me, and I trust the person telling me whether to buy it.

## 2. The IgnitionSim promise

Puzzlewick sells the wonder of owning the object, then proves it is real.
IgnitionSim sells the world the reader wants to inhabit, then proves the room,
hardware, software, ergonomics, and setup tax can support that world.

Every article follows four verbs:

1. **Dream:** show the simulator experience worth building.
2. **Diagnose:** identify the constraint that actually controls the purchase.
3. **Prove:** use real products, installations, screenshots, manuals, and
   repeated owner patterns.
4. **Act:** give the reader a buy, caveat, wait, skip, or try-first path.

## 3. Definition of done

### Editorial floor

- One answer-first verdict near the top: buy now, buy with caveats, wait, skip,
  or try first.
- A named audience and decision. The article says who should buy, who should
  not, and which condition changes the verdict.
- A complete system map: room/rig, controls or sensor, display, PC/console,
  mounts, power/USB, software/subscriptions, and service access where relevant.
- A budget or seriousness ladder with at least three useful paths.
- One explicit "do not buy this yet" judgment.
- One installation or commissioning sequence that names the real work.
- One "what owners love" and one "what makes people return it" synthesis.
- Beginner guidance, intermediate tuning, and advanced or edge-case advice.
- Four or more useful FAQs written as direct answers, not keyword padding.
- At least eight sources for a Tier A cornerstone, including official product
  evidence, official manuals/support where available, expert review evidence,
  and more than one community or owner signal.
- A reviewed official manual or setup document for every major product when one
  is publicly available. Pull out the ports, mount sequence, calibration order,
  included cable/fastener list, and the warning most likely to cost a weekend.
- A clearly labeled `parts to have before installation` checklist and a
  `commissioning order` assembled from official documentation plus repeated
  owner patterns. Never turn one forum failure into a universal defect.
- No fabricated quote, star rating, review count, test result, ownership claim,
  firmware behavior, price, compatibility statement, or firsthand experience.

### Visual floor

- A purpose-made 1200x675 product-first thumbnail.
- A different 1200x630 social card and 1080x1350 vertical share asset.
- A distinct immersive hero that sells the correct simulator world.
- Twelve or more useful visuals for a Tier A cornerstone; eight for a normal
  certified guide.
- At least six real evidence visuals: exact product, installed product, person
  using/installing it, part closeup, official UI/screenshot, or manual/support
  proof.
- Show official box/packaging and a legible manual/setup page for each lead
  product when attributable media can be found. If either is genuinely absent,
  record the search in the research dossier and state what official
  contents/documentation was verified instead. Never generate a substitute box
  or manual.
- At least two practical diagrams for a Tier A cornerstone. Good examples:
  room geometry, load path, USB/power tree, display angle, throw ratio, mounting
  points, control map, upgrade ladder, or installation timeline.
- At least one large curator action scene where the curator is doing useful
  work in the correct simulator world.
- No fake hardware, fake game UI, fake telemetry, generic placeholder,
  postage-stamp proof wall, or repeated image pretending to be variety.
- Product and inspection images render uncropped and remain identifiable on a
  390px mobile viewport.

### Curator floor

- The responsible curator owns the subject based on
  `docs/CURATOR-BIBLE-IGNITIONSIM.md`.
- The article includes at least three distinct curator moments: a field
  briefing, a decision or warning, and a next-step or sign-off.
- The curator is measuring, installing, tuning, driving, flying, swinging,
  wiring, comparing, or diagnosing. Standing beside a product is not a job.
- Each appearance changes action, camera angle, emotional beat, location, and
  lighting. A background swap does not create a new moment.
- The curator translates sourced evidence into memorable judgment. They never
  claim to own, test, or physically review the product.
- Robert Pruitt remains the accountable author/editor in visible copy and
  schema. Curators are clearly disclosed AI-assisted editorial personas.

### Commerce and publishing floor

- Commercial topic selection begins with a monetization check. Every primary
  ranked or recommended product needs a verified exact Amazon ASIN unless an
  alternate seller's affiliate program is confirmed active and configured in
  the site. Direct-only or unavailable products may remain as necessary
  technical context, but they do not displace an equally useful monetizable
  recommendation.
- Amazon CTAs use the exact product URL:
  `https://www.amazon.com/dp/ASIN?tag=ignitionsim-20`.
- A source link is labeled as evidence. It is not disguised as a buy button.
- If the exact Amazon product cannot be verified, omit the retail CTA and leave
  the product name as editorial text. Manufacturer, manual, forum, reviewer, or
  specialist sources may appear only as explicitly labeled research evidence.
  Until Robert approves another affiliate relationship, no purchase path,
  clickable product-name shopping link, affiliate link, or buy-style CTA may
  lead anywhere except an exact Amazon product page.
- Include the standard save/share dock with exact-page Pinterest media, native
  share when supported, Reddit, X, copy-link, and `share_click` analytics.
- Frontmatter dates, `goldStatus`, certification date, source review date,
  revenue tier, cluster, author, FAQ, related guides, and image fields are
  correct.
- The guide appears in its bay, `/guides`, Sim Stream, curator profile, related
  routes, sitemap, RSS, and the relevant game/software hub. Homepage promotion
  remains quality-gated; every new guide that clears the discovery gate enters
  the homepage's newest-ten Sim Stream rail until newer certified work replaces it.
- Desktop and 390px mobile QA pass with no overflow, crop, blank image,
  unreadable infographic, intrusive overlay, or broken transformed image.
- Gold, public-surface, curator-system, editorial-surface, quality, commerce,
  image, and build checks pass before push.

## 4. Research process: build the evidence map first

Do not draft from search snippets. Make a dossier before prose.

### Pass A: demand and builder questions

Collect current questions from category Reddit communities, specialist forums,
manufacturer support pages, retailer questions, video transcripts/comments,
owner reviews, and search suggestions. Group them into:

- first purchase and upgrade order;
- compatibility and ecosystem lock-in;
- room, desk, rig, mount, and service fit;
- PC/console, USB, power, firmware, and software requirements;
- installation time and specialist tools;
- subscriptions, licenses, replacement parts, and true total cost;
- repeated failures, returns, regrets, and workarounds;
- what the product changes in actual driving, flying, swinging, sailing, or
  spaceflight;
- what experienced owners would buy differently.

The outline follows these jobs, not the order in which sources were found.

### Pass B: primary-source truth

Build a fact sheet from the manufacturer product page, manual, support portal,
compatibility chart, firmware notes, and installation PDF:

- exact model, generation, region, and included parts;
- dimensions, weight, mounting pattern, power, ports, platform support, and
  software requirements;
- setup/calibration sequence and required service clearance;
- exact included contents, plus the tools, cables, adapters, fasteners, mounts,
  hubs, power capacity, and replacement parts the buyer may still need;
- supported games/platforms and documented exclusions;
- warranty and return constraints when officially published;
- current MSRP only when explicitly sourced and labeled as MSRP.

### Pass C: expert and owner synthesis

Look for repeated patterns across several credible voices:

- what remains valuable after the honeymoon;
- where setup takes longer than the sales page implies;
- common noise, flex, heat, disconnect, shadow, tracking, or calibration issues;
- how much the part changes control feel or immersion;
- whether a cheaper product solves the same job;
- what owners wish they had measured or purchased first.
- which install steps fail first, which USB or firmware conflicts recur, which
  optional parts become effectively mandatory, and which troubleshooting order
  prevents blind driver reinstalls or unnecessary returns.

Label working notes as `official fact`, `expert observation`, `owner pattern`,
or `editorial inference`. Community evidence can reveal a pattern, but it does
not overrule current official compatibility documentation.

### Pass D: image and rights ledger

Record the source URL, direct asset URL, exact model, visual role, rights basis,
credit, dimensions, and local filename before the image enters the article.
Use `docs/SOURCE-LEXICON-IGNITIONSIM.md` and `docs/REAL-PHOTOS.md`.

### Pass E: commerce verification

Confirm the monetizable destination before drafting, then open every CTA.
Confirm model, edition/generation, platform, bundle, region, and ASIN. Null
stale prices unless an approved live feed supplies them. Run
`npm run audit:commerce`; a monetized link to a manufacturer or specialty store
is a release failure, not a harmless fallback.

## 5. Writing process: verdict, system, friction, experience

### Write the verdict first

Good:

> Buy the ActivePedal Pro when profile-driven brake behavior is the point and
> the rig is already rigid. Buy the mBooster when you want most of the active
> feedback at a lower system cost. Buy neither until the pedal deck stops moving.

Weak:

> Both products have advantages, and the best option depends on your needs.

### Use answer-first headings

Typical sequence:

1. Should you buy it?
2. What are you really building around it?
3. Will it fit your room, rig, PC, and platform?
4. What arrives in the box and what is still missing?
5. How difficult is installation and calibration?
6. What changes when you actually use it?
7. What do experienced owners love?
8. What makes people return it?
9. Which alternative fits a different builder?
10. What should you buy now?

Each section begins with the answer, then proves it with facts, tradeoffs, and
an observable action. Avoid catalog prose and generic superlatives.

### Translate specifications into consequences

- Torque becomes frame load, clipping headroom, heat, and fatigue.
- Projector throw becomes mount location, shadow risk, and swing clearance.
- Launch-monitor technology becomes minimum room depth and left/right-hand fit.
- USB devices become port count, power budget, cable route, and profile recovery.
- Active pedals become power, software profiles, mounting rigidity, and service
  access.

## 6. The proof stack

Use images for different reader jobs:

| Role | Evidence | Reader question |
|---|---|---|
| Recognition | Exact product/front/box | Is this the right model? |
| Ownership | Contents, ports, mounts, accessories | What arrives and what is missing? |
| Installation | Real mounted product or setup sequence | How does this go into my room or rig? |
| Use | Real person driving/flying/swinging/installing | What does ownership feel like? |
| Software | Official UI, settings, game screenshot | What must I configure? |
| Decision | Original diagram or scorecard | Which branch fits me? |
| Relationship | Curator action scene | Who is guiding me and what do they notice? |

Source order:

1. Manufacturer press kit, product page, manual, support, or official CDN.
2. Official game/software media and screenshots.
3. Approved specialty retailer or reviewer media with rights/credit clarity.
4. Licensed community/owner photography with explicit permission or license.
5. Pexels, Unsplash, Wikimedia, Openverse, or other reusable licensed media for
   people, rooms, and atmosphere.
6. Existing verified IgnitionSim assets.
7. Amazon images remain hotlink-only and linked to the product under Amazon's
   terms. Do not download and rehost them as editorial proof.

For every lead product, the image ledger records `box found`, `manual found`,
`installed/use found`, and `parts/ports found`. A missing role needs a written
search note and the best honest substitute from official documentation; it does
not receive generated evidence.

## 7. Generated imagery and hybrid composites

Generated imagery creates dream, emotion, setting, and curator continuity. It
never replaces a real component, control, cockpit, launch monitor, projector,
screen, game UI, telemetry display, cable path, or manual page.

Every prompt specifies:

1. curator identity reference;
2. useful action;
3. emotion/body language;
4. specific simulator-world setting;
5. camera height, lens feeling, crop, and depth;
6. lighting and palette;
7. negative space for real product compositing;
8. reality guardrails.

### Variation rule

No two generated images in one article may repeat more than two of: action,
camera angle, crop, location, time of day, key-light color, expression, people
count, foreground object, or visual rhythm.

### Product-first thumbnail prompt pattern

```text
Create a bright, realistic 16:9 editorial environment for IgnitionSim using
the supplied curator identity reference. Show [CURATOR] on the LEFT THIRD,
actively [MEASURING / INSTALLING / TUNING] in a specific [PIT GARAGE / GOLF BAY /
COCKPIT / COMMAND STATION / BRIDGE]. Leave a clean CENTER-RIGHT stage for the
exact real product photograph to be composited later. [EMOTION], [CAMERA],
[LIGHTING], realistic ergonomics and room scale. Do not draw hardware, logos,
screens, telemetry, labels, cables, or product text. No watermark, collage,
floating objects, empty stare, or glamor pose.
```

Composite the exact product pixels afterward. Remove the studio-white surround,
match perspective and contact shadow, preserve labels, and inspect at 300x169.
Label nearby lifestyle composites `AI editorial scene + real product composite`.

### Curator action-scene prompt pattern

```text
Use the supplied [CURATOR] identity reference. Show them actively [TASK] with a
second adult builder in a lived-in [SIM WORLD]. The action must reveal a useful
decision: [LOAD PATH / ROOM GEOMETRY / USB POWER / CONTROL BINDING / HELM AXIS].
Candid documentary moment, [CAMERA/LENS], [TIME/LIGHT], readable hands and tools,
realistic workstation scale, expressive but restrained body language. Omit all
branded hardware and screens unless supplied as unchanged real reference pixels.
No fake UI, telemetry, logo, text, watermark, pasted-on portrait, or pose-only
composition.
```

Reject an image when the curator is decorative, the product is hallucinated,
screens are fake, the scene is too dark to inspect, ergonomics are impossible,
the same pose appears elsewhere, or the frame could serve another article by
changing one noun.

## 8. Curator behavior

The curators are adult editorial hosts, not mascots. They provide:

- a page-aware short answer;
- one memorable warning;
- a diagnostic or measurement the reader can perform now;
- a personal origin or shop story that explains their bias;
- a current rig and current obsession;
- a next useful guide;
- disagreement when another curator would weigh the trade differently.

Chat, article field briefings, Sim Stream notes, homepage interruptions, and
profiles draw from `src/data/crew.json`, `src/data/crew-conversations.json`, and
`src/data/editorial-surfaces.json`. See `docs/CURATOR-BIBLE-IGNITIONSIM.md`.

## 9. Publishing and quality sequence

For new or materially refreshed articles:

```bash
npm run audit:quality
npm run gold-audit
npm run build
```

Then perform browser QA at approximately 1440x1000 and 390x844:

- inspect every lazy image at full size;
- confirm no horizontal overflow;
- verify the thumbnail in homepage, Sim Stream, bay, curator, guide shelf, and
  related cards;
- verify every newly certified discovery-grade article appears in the homepage
  newest-ten rail and is not silently displaced by score-only sorting;
- verify product images are exact, uncropped, and not repeated as false variety;
- test the curator field briefing and page-aware chat;
- click every retail CTA and confirm the exact product;
- verify sitemap, RSS, canonical, article metadata, and game-hub placement.

After push, watch Netlify to `ready`, verify the production commit and live
URL, repeat mobile/image checks against the image CDN, then submit IndexNow.

## 10. New-standard enforcement date

Articles certified or materially recertified on or after **2026-07-15** must
meet v2. Older certified pages are grandfathered only to avoid taking the site
offline; they remain in a revenue-weighted rescue queue and do not establish a
precedent for new work.

V2 also locks the following inline-proof gate:

- Do not use `.shotgrid` or any equivalent micro-collage in a certified guide.
  Product, installation, control, and use evidence must appear individually
  beside the decision it supports.
- Render `.proof-wide` images at their natural ratio with `object-fit: contain`;
  never crop a product, connector, mounting diagram, or setup to fill a frame.
- Include at least four distinct curator actions: orient the build, measure or
  install something, challenge a weak assumption, and demonstrate the finished
  simulator experience.
- Label AI-assisted scenes honestly and never use them as product proof.
- Owner evidence must be allowed to change the verdict, not merely decorate an
  opinion already written.
- Tier A buyer guides require at least two practical, legible diagrams in
  addition to real product and use photography.
- Failures move to `goldStatus: "refresh"`; `npm run gold-audit` must pass before
  certification is restored.

The build-level curator audit applies immediately to every crew member. New v2
article checks apply by certification date so the gate is strict without
pretending the legacy backlog is already repaired.

## 11. Automatic failures

- Marketplace-style product grid with little judgment.
- Wrong product, Amazon search link, generic "check price" CTA, or unverified
  bundle.
- Fake hardware, fake screen, fake telemetry, fake manual, or fake installation.
- Dark AI room with a tiny white-background product pasted on top.
- Repeated curator pose or portrait sticker.
- Curator language with no evidence, diagnostic, or decision.
- Tiny collage, skinny/tall card, unreadable diagram, cropped product, or wall
  of text followed by an unrelated gallery.
- Specifications without system consequences.
- Owner consensus inferred from one comment.
- Placeholder text, procedural art, empty modules, or template labels on a live
  surface.
- Publication without bay, Stream, curator, related, game, sitemap, RSS, and
  homepage-promotion checks where applicable.

## 12. Operating rule

**Sell the simulator world, identify the constraint that controls it, prove the
hardware and setup reality with the strongest real evidence, let a useful
curator turn that evidence into memorable judgment, and do not publish until
every surface and every purchase path has been verified.**
