# IgnitionSim Horizon Goal

## Objective
Build IgnitionSim into a $10,000/month authority site for simulator builders who want to buy real hardware quickly, install it cleanly, and get back to playing.

## What The Mature Site Looks Like
- Five clear bays: Racing, Golf, Flight, Space, and Marine.
- Golf and Racing drive the first revenue wave because they combine high purchase intent with higher average order values.
- Flight, Space, and Marine preserve the broader "sim builder lab" identity and create long-tail authority.
- Public content is certified-only: useful, visual, sourced, monetizable, and written for builders rather than content volume.
- Gear pages, best-of rankings, buyer maps, source shelves, and FAQs all reinforce each other.
- Tools stay secondary until their data is strong enough to reduce purchase uncertainty better than an article can.

## Revenue Model
- Affiliate first: Amazon for accessories and broad product coverage, then specialty programs for golf simulator rooms, racing rigs, flight hardware, and electronics.
- Display ads after search traffic is strong enough to avoid cluttering a fragile early site.
- Newsletter sponsorship after the weekly list has real subscribers and a consistent open rate.
- Direct brand placements only after the site has earned trust. Editorial verdicts stay blunt: buy, buy with caveats, wait, or skip.

## Operating Targets
- Work toward 75,000 to 150,000 monthly sessions as the traffic base for $10,000/month potential.
- Target a blended revenue mix around affiliate, display ads, newsletter sponsorship, and direct placements.
- Prioritize pages with high buyer intent, high AOV, recurring confusion, and clear product photos.
- Treat every article as either a revenue page, an authority page, or a support page. If it is none of those, it waits.

## Decision Filter
Before publishing, ask:
- Does this help a builder decide what to buy, avoid, or wait on?
- Does it use real product photos, official images, useful diagrams, or clearly credited real-world imagery?
- Does it contain source-backed warnings, setup tax, compatibility traps, and live review checks?
- Does it support an internal cluster and a monetization path?
- Would this move IgnitionSim closer to the $10,000/month horizon?
