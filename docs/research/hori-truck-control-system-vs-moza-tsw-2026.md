# Research Dossier: HORI Truck Control System vs MOZA TSW/R5 (2026)

Reviewed: 2026-07-15

## Opportunity

IgnitionSim had no dedicated truck-simulation cornerstone despite American Truck Simulator and Euro Truck Simulator 2 supporting a large, active hardware-buying community. Current 2026 Reddit questions repeatedly ask whether HORI's complete truck control system is worth its price versus a modular MOZA TSW/R5 build. This fills a content-variety gap without duplicating the site's wheelbase ladders.

## Editorial Verdict

- MOZA wins steering fidelity, materials, tuning, multi-sim use, and long-term expansion.
- HORI wins complete dedicated-truck workflow: stalks, three pedals, shifter, range/splitter, and physical input density in one package.
- An existing Logitech wheel is the honest third lane for a new player proving the habit.
- Full-system cost and desk depth matter more than comparing headline bundle prices.

## Accuracy Checks

- HORI's marketing input counts vary depending on whether axes and switches are counted separately. The article avoids a false total comparison and uses the official 39-wheel-input statement only for the wheel.
- MOZA's standard bundle includes two pedals, not a clutch. Shifter and stalks are optional.
- Both systems are described as PC-first. No console compatibility is implied.
- HORI Driver 1.18 and shifter-firmware warning was visible on the current product page at review time.
- An older indexed MOZA TSW manual PDF path did not resolve; the article points readers to the current support portal instead.
