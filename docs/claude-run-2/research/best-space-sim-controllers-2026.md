## Community pattern notes (paraphrased in our voice)
- The most-repeated newbie regret is buying ONE stick to "try HOSAS" — the off-hand variant is always out of stock, so people fly single-stick, form a bad impression of two-stick flying, and bounce. Commit to the pair.
- Nobody regrets a Gladiator; buyers who jump straight to a Gunfighter pair without flying a Gladiator first usually admit the Gladiator would have been "good enough." Climb the ladder, don't skip it.
- Stock scarcity is the real spec — VKB grips and Virpil bases sell in waves and vanish for weeks; WinWing is the reliable in-stock fallback at similar money.
- Config software is a learning curve, not an afterthought (VKBDevCfg / VPC / SimAppPro); SimAppPro draws the most complaints.

## Credible warnings / caveats
- Buying two right-hand sticks is the classic HOSAS mistake — buy ambidextrous-twice (T.16000M) or a dedicated L + R pair (VKB Gladiator, Virpil ALPHA). A handed grip in the wrong hand is uncomfortable within an hour.
- Light tabletop baseplates slide under aggressive combat input; a Monstertech mount per stick is not optional. A HOSAS needs two.
- Boutique premium bases (Gunfighter Mk.IV, WarBRD-D) are BASE ONLY — a grip adds $130+; price the whole rig before falling in love.
- Star Citizen has 200+ binds; no stick solves this in hardware — a labeled macro panel (Stream Deck) is the community fix.
