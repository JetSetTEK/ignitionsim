## Community pattern notes (paraphrased in our voice)
- The dominant mounting mistake isn't the wrong bracket — it's a one-yoke owner buying a cockpit frame, or a multi-panel builder skipping the powered USB hub. Match the mount to the gear.
- "My panel vanished and came back" is almost never a dead panel: it's a device browning out on an overloaded/unpowered hub, or Windows suspending the USB port between sessions.
- Older Saitek panels are fussy on USB 3.x ports; USB 2.0 + powered hub + power-management-off is the repeated fix.
- Lightweight sticks twist under load on a bare desk; a rigid aluminum arm (Monstertech-style) is the honest middle rung before a full frame.

## Credible warnings / caveats
- Cranking a clamp on a thin/veneered/overhanging desk edge cracks the desk long before the clamp fails — pad it, clamp a solid section, or use a suction pad / under-desk plate.
- A full HOTAS needs TWO aluminum desk mounts (stick + throttle), so a "$100 mount" is really ~$200 for a complete setup.
- FFB yokes (MOZA AY210, ~9 Nm roll / ~210 N pitch) cannot be held by a desk-edge clamp — they rock the desk or work loose; a rigid clamp, cockpit, or 8020 rig is mandatory.
- Prices are sale-dependent (Monstertech runs frequent sales; Trak Racer / DIY 8020 vary by region and parts) — flagged "check current" throughout.
