## Community pattern notes (paraphrased in our voice)

- The recurring winter forum thread: a buyer reads glowing overhead-camera reviews, mounts a ceiling unit, then discovers the cameras can't focus in an 8.5-ft basement. No mounting trick recovers it — the throw needs the height. Upvoted reply is always "buy the sensor your ceiling can feed; side-mount or nothing."
- "Do a real swing, not a careful one" for the driver test — the extra inches of arc only appear when you stop babying the club.
- Floor-mounted projectors get destroyed by a stray ball/club if not enclosed — treated as the #1 low-ceiling rookie mistake.

## Credible warnings / caveats

- Many builder guides AND floor-unit manufacturers (SkyTrak ST MAX, Foresight GC3) spec a **9-ft minimum** — 8.5 ft is the honest real-world floor, not a comfort zone. Article states this plainly rather than overselling 8.5 ft.
- Overhead camera units (Uneekor EYE XO2, Foresight Falcon) physically cannot focus below their 9-10 ft / 9'6" clearance windows — never recommend for a low room.
- Any floor-mounted (ultra-short-throw) projector MUST live in a protective enclosure.
- Bushnell Launch Pro's full sim/club data is subscription-gated (Silver ~$199/yr, Gold ~$500/yr) — run total cost of ownership, don't compare on sticker alone.
- Photometric cameras need consistent lighting; flagged for SkyTrak+.
