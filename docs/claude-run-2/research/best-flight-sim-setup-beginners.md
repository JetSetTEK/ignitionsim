## Community pattern notes (paraphrased in our voice)
- The most-repeated beginner regret is "right brand, wrong airplane" — a Honeycomb Alpha bought by an Airbus flyer sits unused far more often than any brand disappoints. Name the airplane before the budget.
- The second-most-repeated: buying rudder pedals on day one, then flying twist-rudder for months anyway. Pedals are a "when a maneuver demands it" purchase, not a day-one one.
- Fly the free gamepad first — MSFS 2024's assistance modes make it genuinely flyable, and a week on it cheaply reveals GA-vs-airliner lean, which decides every later purchase.

## Credible warnings / caveats
- Yokes (Logitech, Honeycomb) clamp to a solid desk edge; a flimsy desk or thin lip flexes and creaks under the pull.
- Twist rudder cross-couples with roll on some sticks — that's the cue to add pedals, not a defect to power through forever.
- Console vs PC splits options hard: Xbox players cannot use the PC-only Logitech yoke or pedals; their path is VelocityOne, the Xbox-licensed TCA Yoke Pack, or (soon) the TCA Sky Yoke.
- TCA Sky Yoke is announced, not shipping — treat all its specs/price as pre-launch until retail units and reviews land.
- Every new device needs a calibration session (deadzone/sensitivity) before it feels right — budget the time.
