## Community pattern notes (paraphrased in our voice)
- Consistent across rally/drift communities: buy the handbrake before the shifter; it's the highest-impact control after pedals.
- Load-cell is the upgrade people most often wish they'd bought first for serious drift/rally.
- The single most common "my new handbrake feels terrible" thread ends with the owner finding their mount was flexing, not the hardware failing.
- Drift drivers treat handbrake repeatability the way circuit racers treat brake repeatability — foundation, not luxury.

## Credible warnings / caveats
- A handbrake pulled hard and a shifter slammed into gear load the mount far more than a wheel does — a desk clamp on particle board rarely survives it. Zero-flex aluminum-profile or steel cockpit arm required.
- H-pattern without a clutch pedal is half an experience — budget a three-pedal set with it.
- Most sim handbrakes/shifters are PC-only; TH8A (shifter) and Fanatec ClubSport Handbrake V2 are the notable cross-platform exceptions.
- The load-cell handbrake tier moved a lot in late 2025 (Fanatec V2 launch) — treat every price as a starting point.
- MOZA HBP is an angle/Hall sensor, NOT a load cell — genuinely good, but not the repeatability ceiling. Don't market it as load-cell.
