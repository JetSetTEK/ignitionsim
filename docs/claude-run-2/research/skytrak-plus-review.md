## Headline finding (changes the whole article)

The old draft's thesis — "no-subscription king" + "software freedom (rare TGC 2019 + GSPro + E6)" — is **stale for 2026**. Two verified changes rewrote the angle:

1. **The SkyTrak+ is discontinued.** SkyTrak's own product page lists it as "no longer available," selling as closeout stock (~$1,795–$1,995, was $2,995). The live, in-production unit is the **ST MAX ($2,995)**.
2. **SkyTrak dropped official third-party software support for NEW buyers** (GSPro / E6 Connect / TGC 2019), steering them to its own paid Course Play platform. Existing owners appear grandfathered; the community runs unofficial connectors (OpenSkyPlus-style / GSPro open API via Discord) that are unsupported and can break. This is a **check-current** item, not a clean yes/no — flagged in-body.

## Community pattern notes (paraphrased in our voice, no long quotes)

- Longtime owners still praise the small-room fit and the genuinely useful free driving range.
- The mood shifted once SkyTrak pulled official third-party support for new buyers; the recurring worry is future GSPro/E6 connectivity on a newly bought unit, not the hardware itself.
- Owners report the unofficial GSPro API bridge "works great," but treat it as unsupported and fragile.
- Repeated pragmatic advice: settle your software plan BEFORE buying, and budget Core (~$300/yr) if you want course play.

## Credible warnings / caveats

- **Discontinued hardware:** buying a SkyTrak+ in 2026 = closeout/end-of-life inventory (support horizon shrinking).
- **Software policy is the load-bearing risk:** confirm in writing with the retailer whether a NEW unit connects to any third-party sim you require. Do not assume the legacy "software freedom" pitch applies.
- **No angle of attack; barebones putting; outdoors wants a mat, not grass.**
- **ST MAX premium (~$1,000) buys support/USB-C/speed-training, NOT an accuracy jump** — don't overpay expecting better data.
