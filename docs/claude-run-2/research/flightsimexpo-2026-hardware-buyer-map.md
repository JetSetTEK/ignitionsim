## Community pattern notes (paraphrased in our voice)
- FSExpo-season pattern: viewers get dazzled by the show floor and ask whether to "wait for everything." The repeated correct answer is to buy the one reveal matching your airplane and let the rest ship + get reviewed first.
- AB6-vs-AB9 pattern: combat pilots are happy with the AB6 and never miss the torque; airliner pilots who bought AB6 to save money regret it the first time a hand-flown ILS exposes the cheaper base's cogging.
- Warthog MKII pattern: existing Warthog owners debate whether an electronics/durability refresh justifies ~$600; consensus tilts toward "only if buying your first metal HOTAS or your old one is failing."

## Credible warnings / caveats
- MOZA AB9/AB6 servos need a rigid 4-bolt mount or cockpit frame — a light desk clamp lets the FFB fight the desk.
- Most enthusiast FSExpo reveals (MOZA flight FFB, WinWing panels) are PC-only; Xbox/PS5 buyers are limited to licensed gear (Sky Yoke, VelocityOne).
- Warthog MKII is a refresh, not a torque/FFB upgrade — don't upgrade a healthy original for it.
- Announced-but-unshipped pricing (Sky Yoke $199.99 Q3 2026; Warthog MKII Sept 2026) should be treated as watchlist; verify retail durability and price.
- Multi-panel builds need a powered USB hub (~$60) once past two panels or devices drop mid-flight.
