## Community pattern notes (paraphrased in our voice)
- The recurring r/flightsim buy-timing answer to "when do I need pedals" is some version of "the day a crosswind landing makes you swear at your monitor." Nobody regrets waiting; plenty regret the day-one purchase that sat in the box.
- MFG Crosswind V3 carries an unusually unanimous "buy once, cry once" reputation — owners arrive from budget sliders chasing precision, add the hydraulic damper, and stop shopping. Veterans keep pointing newcomers to it.
- The toe-brake debate resolves the same way every time: brakes for GA/taildragger/crosswind; skip them only for rudder-only combat/warbird flying (VKB T-Rudder territory).

## Credible warnings / caveats
- Heavy metal pedals (Charlie, MFG Crosswind, WinCTRL Orion 32, TPR) walk on carpet under braking — require spikes, a mat, a seat-rail brace, or a cockpit frame.
- Budget sliders (Logitech G 9-bit/7-bit, TFRP 1024-value rudder) use pot-class axes; fine for GA but the first thing to age/drift.
- New WinCTRL FFB pedals are heavy powered devices with maturing software — need a rigid mount and a spare outlet.
- Console (Xbox) flyers have essentially two options: Turtle Beach VelocityOne and Thrustmaster TFRP. Confirm current console support before buying anything else.
- Prices for WinCTRL (Orion 32 + CyberTaurus) and MFG shift by region/sale/store — all flagged "check current."
