## Community pattern notes (paraphrased in our voice)

- The recurring Honeycomb-path thread in 2026 is not "which piece is best" — it's people who paid for a Charlie pre-order and waited indefinitely. Consensus: buy the Alpha and Bravo (real, in stock, excellent), and treat rudder pedals as an open shopping decision rather than a brand-loyalty test.
- Nobody regrets the Alpha yoke or the Bravo throttle. The regret is always the pedal that never shipped.
- Staged buying (fly the Alpha solo for weeks first) is the repeated advice: it teaches you what the Bravo's autopilot panel actually does for you and lets you position each piece correctly.

## Credible warnings / caveats

- **Charlie stock is unreliable** — official listing is Out of Stock; some pilot shops list open pre-orders with no firm date; at least one major retailer dropped the Honeycomb line; the three-piece bundle page 404s. Do not chase it; buy an in-stock pedal instead.
- Honeycomb pieces are separate units, not a single rail system — Alpha/Bravo clamp, pedals sit on the floor. A desk mount or cockpit rig helps once all three are in place, especially to stop the yoke walking under hard inputs.
- Complex airliners (Fenix A320, PMDG 737) may need custom binding on the Bravo's autopilot panel; the panel targets GA/light-jet logic.
- Multi-panel stacks need a powered USB hub (~$60) past two panels or devices drop mid-flight.
- Airbus/Boeing flyers should build a parallel deck, not bolt onto the GA Honeycomb path.
