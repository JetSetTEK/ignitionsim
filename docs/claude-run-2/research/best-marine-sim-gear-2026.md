## Community pattern notes (paraphrased in our voice)
- Recurring bridge-builder wisdom: don't ask "which throttle," ask "how many" — twin-screw vessels need two axes, and two daisy-chained flight quadrants cost less than one premium single throttle.
- The DIY-helm friction lesson: the pots and board are easy; making a bare 10k pot hold position is the real work. Bridge Command builders wrap string around the pot shaft under light tension so the lever drags. Sort friction before fabricating the enclosure.
- The bay's cross-pollination truth: plan one shared rig across racing/flight/marine rather than buying marine-specific anything.

## Credible warnings / caveats
- **Analog-axis gate:** many marine titles read keyboard-style left/right only — an analog wheel maps to a binary turn, an analog throttle does nothing, FFB is dead weight. Verify the specific title before buying anything.
- **Force feedback is wasted:** marine titles almost never support FFB; don't pay the G923 premium over the G29 for TrueForce.
- **DIY is a real project:** fabrication, wiring, friction — not an afternoon.
- **VR sailing is sailing-only** and can cause motion discomfort; start in short sessions.
- **Prices move constantly:** every figure is "check current"; several (G29 street price, MarineVerse model, Prof Ship Sim input specs, RailDriver live price) are explicitly flagged in the body.
