## Community pattern notes (paraphrased in our voice)
- Owners who skip the Gladiator and jump straight to a Gunfighter pair frequently admit the Gladiator would have been "good enough." Start on the cheap magnetic stick; earn the tunable base by knowing what you want to change.
- The Gladiator vs Gunfighter difference is a **feel** difference, not a capability one: fiberglass-infused pincer gimbal with fixed cams (Gladiator) vs all-metal cam gimbal with two springs per cam and four cam profiles (Gunfighter Mk.IV). Adjustability on the Gunfighter is "far superior," per VKB's own forum.
- Grip interchangeability is real but bounded: Rev.C accepts 5 Gunfighter-family grips across Gunfighter bases; a Gladiator's own grip does NOT mount to a Gunfighter base. What carries forward is the VKB ecosystem + VKBDevCfg profiles + muscle memory, not the literal Gladiator grip.
- Recurring advice: buy the Stream Deck / bind solution and mounts before chasing a fancier base — the 200+ bind problem and sliding baseplates hurt more than spring feel.

## Credible warnings / caveats
- VKB sells in production waves; several SKUs (Gladiator SCE, Omni-Throttle, MCG Pro) were on back-in-stock notify at time of writing. All prices are "check current."
- Light, compact Gladiator baseplates slide under combat input; a HOSAS needs two mounts. Non-optional.
- VKBDevCfg has a steep curve; an untuned Gunfighter aims worse than a well-tuned Gladiator — a hardware upgrade you don't tune can feel like a lateral move.
- Do NOT assume the Gladiator grip reuses on a Gunfighter base — the prior version of this article implied a single shared mounting standard across the whole ladder, which overstates the reuse. Corrected here to the Rev.C-grip-catalog reality.
