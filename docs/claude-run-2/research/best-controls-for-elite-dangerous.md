## Community pattern notes (paraphrased in our voice)

- Elite is the outlier space sim where HOTAS holds its own: its flight model leans on pitch/yaw/roll plus a held forward throttle far more than constant lateral strafe, so a detented lever maps cleanly for supercruise, docking, and long exploration. Many veterans fly HOTAS and don't apologize.
- HOSAS earns its keep for FA-off combat and precise fine control (e.g. high-G planetary landings), where analog strafe on the off-hand stick is a genuine edge.
- The recurring FA-off convert pattern: brutal first session, "learning to write with your off hand," then it clicks around the one-week mark and they never go back. People who quit two-stick flying almost always quit inside that first awkward session.
- Post-Operations, the practical add-on tip: run the gamepad (for on-foot) on a *separate* control profile, not layered over flight binds, to avoid triggering ship actions on foot.

## Credible warnings / caveats

- Light tabletop baseplates (T.16000M, compact Gladiator base) slide under aggressive combat input — a Monstertech mount per stick is not optional for HOSAS.
- The ambidextrous-stick trap: buying two right-hand sticks makes a miserable off hand. Buy ambidextrous (T.16000M) twice, or a dedicated L + dedicated R (VKB Gladiator).
- Elite's bind list is deep; mapping six axes plus systems across two sticks is a genuine evening. A Stream Deck / edrefcard reference card is the standard mitigation.
- Operations is a hybrid ship+FPS format; a HOSAS is awkward for on-foot FPS — keep a gamepad within reach.
- Prices move with sales and boutique/stock waves; every figure is a 2026 street estimate — "check current" before checkout.
