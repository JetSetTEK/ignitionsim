## Community pattern notes (paraphrased in our voice)
- The single loudest answer to "what should I upgrade next" after a bundle is pedals, not power. A 2026 r/simracing poll (cited across buyer's guides) put upgrading pedals first with roughly two-thirds of the vote.
- The recurring regret thread: "I bought a bigger base and my lap times didn't move; then I got a load cell and they did." Brake feel (pressure, not travel) is where consistency lives.
- Buy the rig earlier than instinct says. Community and guide consensus is that people who buy a cheap wheel stand first almost always re-buy an 8020 rig later — two rigs instead of one.
- Quick-release / rim lock-in is the durable cost, not the base itself. Pedals and shifters are USB and brand-agnostic and survive every base change.

## Warnings / caveats (surfaced in the article)
- A 12Nm+ base on a desk clamp flexes, creaks, and can crack a desk — this is why the rig (Stage 3) comes before the base upgrade (Stage 4).
- A load cell on a flexing stand throws away the repeatability it was bought for; rigid mount + heel plate required.
- Fanatec QR2 can disable force feedback on non-Fanatec rims — factor in if you own third-party wheels.
- Firmware can change the real torque figure (Fanatec ClubSport DD 12→15Nm) — check current firmware, not the box.
- Several "entry" bases only hit their higher torque with an optional Boost Kit — confirm the power supply in the box.
- ButtKicker Gamer PRO US price not hard-confirmed — body says "~$200+ · check current".
