## Community pattern notes (paraphrased in our voice)
- Choose the MOZA tier your cockpit can rigidly hold and your upgrade path wants, not the biggest torque number your curiosity likes.
- A heavy GT rim on a small base dulls the detail ("steering through pudding") — owners of weighty wheels are steered toward the R9/R12 as much by wheel weight as by torque.
- The historic "don't waste money below an R9/R12" advice used to be spec snobbery; in 2026 it's closer to correct because the R9 V3 price cut shrank the step-up penalty.
- Repeated regret across MOZA threads: bundle pedals are the first thing people wish they'd upgraded — brake feel (load cell) beats extra torque.

## Credible warnings / caveats
- **PC-only.** No console support on the R5 at all; console racers need the R3 (Xbox) or a Fanatec/Logitech PS5 base.
- **Bundle pedals + paddles are the weak link.** SR-P Lite spring brake and ES spring paddles; budget a load-cell brake (or MOZA's ~$29 performance kit stopgap) as the first upgrade.
- **Prices are sale-dependent.** Mid-Year Sale (~Jun 16 – Jul 15, 2026) is actively reshuffling R5/R9/R12 pricing — the right base this week may differ next month.
- **V2 wheels are wired on the R5** (slip-ring), wireless on R9+. Function is the same; only a cable differs.
- **Myth corrected:** the R5 does NOT have an oddball/incompatible mounting pattern (a claim in the prior draft). It's standard 4-hole M6 and lines up with MOZA's plate; a dual desk clamp is in the box.
