## Community pattern notes (paraphrased in our voice)
- The recurring regret isn't "wrong brand," it's "right brand, wrong airplane." Buy for the airframe you fly most.
- Simmers who split GA and airliner time end up owning pieces of both ecosystems; no single ~$500 purchase does both jobs authentically.
- TCA Sidestick is the consensus entry-bracket Airbus value: closest to a real A320neo/Fenix cockpit without premium money.

## Credible warnings / caveats
- The structural ecosystem gap: Honeycomb Bravo has a built-in autopilot panel; the TCA Airbus path does NOT — autopilot lives on a separate FCU/glareshield + MCDU you buy later. Budget for it so the ~$200 Airbus entry doesn't surprise at ~$500 for hardware autopilot.
- A yoke is the wrong control for an Airbus side-stick airliner, and a side-stick is wrong for GA/Boeing — mission mismatch is the top regret.
- TCA Boeing Yoke Pack's bundled quadrant has a spring-back that can let throttle settings drift (the yoke is the star of the pack).
- Honeycomb GA gear config on MSFS 2024 can take a session to bind; X-Plane 12 keybinding is smoother.
