# Opportunity Scout — Part 2 (Flight + Space + Marine + Cross-Sim)

30 opportunities, scored. Distribution: flight 7, space 8, marine 2, cross-sim 13.

## Strongest themes

**1. Force feedback flight went mainstream in 2026 — this is the headline.**
The single biggest news cluster is consumer FFB flight hardware launching at once: MOZA's **AB9** (12 Nm dual-servo, $499) and **AB6** bases with the MH16 stick/MTP throttle, and WinWing (now **WINCTRL**) unveiling the **CyberTaurus** FFB base + Ursa Minor throttle at FSWeekend 2026. These crash into the incumbent Virpil/VKB world and create a genuine ecosystem lock-in decision buyers have never faced before. High AOV, high authority, and mostly low competition because the coverage is brand-new. Recommended pillar: the "which 2026 ecosystem to buy into" page sitting above the individual reviews. Priority-1/2 cluster.

**2. MOZA glass-panel cockpit lineup (Q3 2026) is a wait/buy first-mover play.**
MOZA announced a G1000 panel (MGX1000), Airbus MCDU (MA3F), Boeing FMC (MB7F), and autopilot module at FSExpo/FSWeekend 2026 with a Q3 window and unannounced pricing. Almost no competing buyer content exists yet. Treat as a noindexed lab/wait page until price + real photos land (flagged needsProductPhotoWork), then flip to a strong buy/wait verdict.

**3. Availability/confusion pages win trust cheaply.**
The **Honeycomb Charlie rudder pedals never reached wide release** (Pilot Mall dropped the whole Honeycomb line) — high search, low answer, perfect for a blunt "do not build around it, buy X" verdict. Same class: the WinWing→WINCTRL rebrand confusion, and the Turtle Beach VelocityOne sticky-yoke defect + fix. Low competition, real buyer value.

**4. Cross-sim accessories are the widest, most reusable vein (13 of 30).**
Powered USB hubs (multi-panel dropouts), heavy triple-monitor stands, ultrawide/triple + VRAM reality for MSFS 2024 (16GB stutters, 18-20GB safe), the X3D + high-VRAM PC build sheet, Pimax Crystal Super vs Dream Air vs Quest 3 VR picker, TrackIR/Tobii/DelanClip head tracking, ButtKicker tactile for flight/space, open-back vs comms audio, bias lighting, cable management, and a compact/apartment setups hub. Several overlap existing Tier-2 refresh candidates (USB hub, head tracking, ultrawide) — scope them as refreshes/expansions, not new files, and lean on the flight/space-specific angle to avoid cannibalizing the certified racing triples piece. MOZA's one-app Cockpit software (flight + racing shared profiles) is a real cross-sell hook from the racing installed base.

**5. Space (8) is mostly premium-stick decisioning + post-purchase support.**
The VKB vs Virpil vs WinWing question (currently demoted — rescue it on an ergonomics/reliability angle, the Virpil Alpha hand-fatigue vs VKB pain point is the differentiator), HOSAS desk mounting beyond Monstertech (Mach1 stowable is uncovered), Elite-specific controls (distinct flight model), DCS throttle showdown with the WinWing-QC caveat, and the "now make six axes fly right" curve/bind support page that de-risks the purchase.

**6. Marine stays honest and niche (2).**
NAUTIS Home consumer ship sim + what hardware actually works (pro azimuth levers are industrial/type-approved; most home users run generic USB throttles), and a DIY azimuth/Z-drive twin-lever guide for ASD tug handling. Low AOV, low competition, pure long-tail authority — matches the triage's "marine reality first, DIY where useful" directive.
